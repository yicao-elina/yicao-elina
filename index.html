About Me

I am a passionate and driven PhD candidate at Johns Hopkins University, specializing in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering. My research focuses on the intersection of AI for Science, computer-aided material design, Density Functional Theory (DFT), and Molecular Dynamics (MD). I am particularly interested in advancing the understanding and prediction of degradation in novel materials, with a current focus on 2D thermoelectrics. My goal is to leverage these computational tools to design next-generation materials for a sustainable future.

Recent Work

From Wastes to Watts: Machine-Learned Thermoelectrics

Developing ML-enhanced interatomic potentials to model and optimize Cr-doped Sb₂Te₃/CrTe₂ heterostructures for high-temperature thermoelectric applications.

Unveiling Defect Tolerance in Perovskite Solar Materials

Investigated the self-healing phenomena in metal halide perovskites by combining DFT and transition state analysis to uncover defect migration pathways and enhance material stability.

Computer-Aided Drug Design at Viva Biotech

Conducted co-solvent MD simulations to analyze protein-ligand interactions and optimize the drug discovery pipeline.

Advisor for Fudan's iGEM Team

Guided the experimental design and scientific documentation for the Fudan iGEM team, which won a Gold Medal and the Best Environmental Project title.
